Chihuahuan Desert

The Chihuahuan Desert is a desert, and an ecoregion designation, that straddles the U.S.-Mexico border in the central and northern portions of the Mexican Plateau. You may stay in The United States or Mexico to visit.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon rainforest, also known in English as Amazonia or the Amazon Jungle, is a moist broadleaf forest that covers most of the Amazon basin of South America. You may stay in Brazil, Peru, Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, Bolivia, Guyana, Suriname, and even France (French Guiana) to visit.

Himalayas

The Himalayas or Himalaya (literally meaning "abode of snow") This range is home to nine of the ten highest peaks on Earth, including the highest above sea level, Mount Everest. You may stay in India, Nepal, Bhutan, China (Tibet), or Pakistan to visit.

Great Lakes

The Great Lakes are a series of interconnected freshwater lakes located in northeastern North America, on the Canada–United States border, which connect to the Atlantic Ocean through the Saint Lawrence River. You may stay in The United States or Canada to visit.

Maldives

The Maldives, officially the Republic of Maldives, is an island country and archipelago in the Indian Ocean. It lies southwest of India and Sri Lanka in the Laccadive Sea. The chain of twenty six atolls stretches from Ihavandhippolhu Atoll to the Addu Atoll. The capital and largest city is Malé, traditionally called the "King's Island." And yes the shore does actually turn blue at night. You may stay on any of the many small islands to visit.
